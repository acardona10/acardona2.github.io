Great Lakes

Lake Superior is the largest in surface area and in water volume, it is north of Lake Huron. Lake Michigan is only the third largest of the Great Lakes when measured by water surface. Lake Huron is the second largest Great Lake by surface area and has the longest shoreline. Lake Erie is the fourth largest of the Great Lakes when measured in surface area and the smallest by water volume. Lake Ontario is the smallest of the Great Lakes when measured in surface area. It is much deeper than Erie and holds about four times the water volume.
